About SDG 14

What is Life Under Water?

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 14 (SDG 14) aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. It forms a cornerstone of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

Marine and coastal ecosystems provide critical ecosystem services: food security, livelihoods for coastal communities, climate regulation, and cultural significance across the globe.

  • 14.4 Regulate overfishing and restore fish stocks
  • 14.5 Conserve at least 10% of coastal and marine areas
  • 14.7 Increase economic benefits from sustainable oceans
Key Threats

Challenges Facing Our Oceans

🌡️

Ocean Warming

Rising ocean temperatures disrupt marine ecosystems and bleach coral reefs, threatening biodiversity hotspots.

🧴

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean each year, harming marine wildlife and entering the food chain.

🎣

Overfishing

More than 33% of global fish stocks are harvested at unsustainable levels, pushing many species to the brink.

⚗️

Ocean Acidification

The ocean absorbs 30% of CO₂ produced; rising acidity threatens shell-forming creatures and coral reefs.
